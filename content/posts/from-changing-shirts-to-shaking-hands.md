## I kept a spare shirt in my car. And my desk drawer. And my gym bag.

Not because I was forgetful. Because by 10 AM on most days, the one I was wearing was done.

**I had a system.** Arrive early. Claim the seat closest to the AC vent. Keep my hands in my pockets during introductions. Time bathroom breaks for right before any meeting where I might need to shake someone's hand. Carry a napkin in my right pocket to dry my palm in the three seconds between standing up and extending my hand.

If this sounds exhausting, it was. If it sounds familiar — that's why I'm writing this.

## The decade of engineering

I spent ten years building an invisible architecture around hyperhidrosis. Every room was a thermal assessment. Every social event was a logistics problem. Every outfit was chosen for its ability to hide what was happening underneath.

And the whole time, I was told the same things:
- "Have you tried certain dri?"
- "Just relax"
- "Everyone sweats"

**No. Not everyone sweats through a dress shirt sitting in a 68-degree office doing nothing.**

## What actually changed

I wish I could tell you there was one breakthrough moment. There wasn't. It was a slow accumulation of changes that, together, shifted the baseline.

**Stress management** — not "meditation apps," but actual nervous system training. Breathing protocols that target vagal tone. Cold exposure that teaches your autonomic system a new response pattern.

**Nutrition** — not a diet. Understanding which foods trigger sympathetic activation and which support parasympathetic function. This was bigger than I expected.

**Sleep** — not "8 hours." Understanding sleep architecture, circadian alignment, and how poor sleep keeps your nervous system in overdrive.

**Movement** — not "exercise." Specific types of physical activity that improve autonomic regulation rather than spike it.

## Where I am now

I still sweat. I want to be honest about that. But the baseline is different. The spirals are shorter. The engineering is mostly gone.

**I shake hands without thinking about it.** That sentence means nothing to most people. If you have HH, you know exactly what it means.

This is what lifestyle change looks like for hyperhidrosis. Not a cure. Not a miracle. A fundamentally different relationship with your own nervous system.

**And it started with someone finally telling me: the sweating isn't the problem. The pattern underneath is.**
