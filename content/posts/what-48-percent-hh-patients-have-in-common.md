## You got the promotion. Your first thought was "they'll figure out I don't belong here."

Your second thought was about whether your hands would be dry enough to shake the CEO's hand at the next all-hands meeting.

**48% of hyperhidrosis patients meet the clinical threshold for Imposter Phenomenon.** That's nearly half. And it's not because people with HH are less competent — it's because living with a visible, uncontrollable condition trains your brain to believe you're one handshake away from being exposed.

## The study nobody talks about

Research published in the *Journal of the American Academy of Dermatology* found that hyperhidrosis patients scored significantly higher on the Clance Imposter Phenomenon Scale than the general population.

The researchers noted that the "uncontrollability" of symptoms was the primary driver — not the sweating itself, but the inability to predict or prevent it.

> "The unpredictable nature of hyperhidrosis episodes creates a persistent state of anticipatory anxiety that mirrors the cognitive patterns seen in Imposter Phenomenon."

## How sweating becomes self-doubt

The mechanism is straightforward once you see it:

1. **You can't control a visible symptom** → you develop hypervigilance
2. **Hypervigilance in social settings** → you assume others notice what you notice
3. **Assumed judgment** → you begin attributing your success to luck, timing, or deception
4. **Attribution to deception** → classic imposter cognition

This isn't a personality flaw. It's a predictable psychological consequence of living with an uncontrollable visible condition for years.

## The part that matters

**Treating the sweating alone doesn't resolve the imposter pattern.** By the time someone has lived with HH for a decade, the cognitive patterns are self-sustaining. The sweating planted the seed, but the thought patterns grew roots of their own.

This is why a comprehensive approach matters. You need nervous system work AND cognitive restructuring AND social reintegration. Addressing one without the others leaves the loop intact.

**You're not an imposter. You're someone whose body taught their brain a lie.**
